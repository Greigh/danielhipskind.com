Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 1, 2025

Information We Collect

Adamas is designed with privacy in mind. Most application data, including notes, patterns, settings, and user preferences, remains stored locally on your device using your browser's local storage and is never transmitted to our servers.

We do not collect any personal information from anonymous users. Server-side data collection only occurs when you create and log into an Adamas account for advanced features:

  • User Account Information: Username, email, and encrypted password for authentication purposes
  • Twilio SMS Integration: Account credentials (Account SID, Auth Token, phone number) are stored securely on our servers for SMS functionality, but only when you explicitly configure this feature

All integration credentials are encrypted and associated only with your specific user account. You can view, modify, or delete this information at any time through your account settings, or delete your account entirely to remove all server-stored data.

Anonymous Usage

You can use Adamas without creating an account or logging in. When used anonymously:

  • No personal information is collected or stored
  • No data is transmitted to our servers
  • All functionality works entirely offline
  • Advanced features like SMS integration are not available
  • Your privacy is completely protected

Anonymous usage provides full access to core call center tools including number formatting, call logging, notes, timers, and basic settings.

Local Storage Usage

The application uses browser local storage (which never leaves your browser) to save your:

  • Number Formatter Data: Custom formatting patterns for account numbers, phone numbers, Social Security Numbers (SSNs), and other sensitive numerical data
  • Call notes and conversation records
  • Application settings and preferences
  • Timer configurations
  • Theme selections
  • Other user interface customizations

This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third parties. For anonymous users, this is the only data storage used.

Integration credentials (such as Twilio API keys) are only stored securely on our servers when you are logged into a Call Center Helper account and explicitly configure these advanced features through the authenticated settings interface.

Number Formatter and Sensitive Data

The Number Formatter feature allows you to create and apply custom formatting patterns to various types of sensitive numerical data. This includes:

  • Phone Numbers: Formatting for domestic and international numbers
  • Account Numbers: Bank accounts, credit cards, customer IDs
  • Social Security Numbers (SSNs): US Social Security Numbers
  • Other Sensitive Data: Tax IDs, license numbers, policy numbers, etc.

Privacy Protection: All formatting patterns and the data you input for formatting remain stored locally on your device only. No sensitive numerical data or formatting patterns are ever transmitted to our servers or third parties. The Number Formatter operates entirely offline and within your browser's secure environment.

You are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws (such as HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA) when handling sensitive information through the Number Formatter. We recommend using this feature only with data you have proper authorization to process.

CRM Integration and API Keys

Call Center Helper supports integration with various CRM systems (Cisco Finesse, Five9, Salesforce, Zendesk, HubSpot, and Microsoft Dynamics 365). When you configure CRM connections:

  • API keys and credentials are stored locally in your browser
  • Sensitive information is never transmitted to our servers
  • You can disconnect and clear stored credentials at any time
  • Demo mode is available when CRM systems are unreachable

All CRM API calls are made directly from your browser to the CRM provider's servers. We do not proxy, log, or store any CRM data.

SMS and Twilio Integration

Call Center Helper supports SMS messaging through Twilio integration for authenticated users only. This feature is not available to anonymous users. When you log into your Call Center Helper account and configure SMS functionality:

  • Your Twilio Account SID, Auth Token, and phone number are stored securely on our servers, encrypted and associated only with your user account
  • SMS message content is transmitted directly to Twilio's servers for delivery and is not stored or logged by us
  • Phone numbers you send SMS messages to are processed temporarily during message delivery and are not retained after transmission
  • You have full control to configure, modify, or remove your Twilio credentials at any time through the Settings page
  • SMS functionality requires your explicit setup and cannot be used without your Twilio account configuration

All SMS communications are handled securely through Twilio's encrypted API. We do not have access to your SMS message content, delivery status, or recipient information beyond what is necessary for the immediate transmission process.

Cookies and Tracking

We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, analytics services, or any form of user tracking. Your browsing activity is not monitored or recorded by us.

Data Security

Since all data is stored locally on your device, security depends on your device's security measures. We recommend:

  • Using strong device passwords
  • Keeping your browser and operating system updated
  • Using antivirus software
  • Being cautious with shared devices

Cloudflare Services

We use Cloudflare to provide content delivery network (CDN) services, DDoS protection, and web security for our application. When you access Call Center Helper:

  • Cloudflare may collect standard web server logs including IP addresses, browser information, and access timestamps
  • This information is used solely for security, performance, and analytics purposes to protect and optimize our service
  • Cloudflare's data collection and privacy practices are governed by their own Privacy Policy
  • We have configured Cloudflare to minimize data collection and respect user privacy
  • Cloudflare data is not combined with any personal information we may collect from authenticated users

Cloudflare helps ensure the security and reliability of our service while maintaining your privacy. Their services do not affect the local storage of your data or our server-side data collection policies.

Children's Privacy

This application is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.
